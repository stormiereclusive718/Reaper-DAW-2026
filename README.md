## 🎧 First Time Setup

Open the application from your desktop shortcut to configure your audio settings:

1.  Select Options from the main menu bar.
2.  Click Preferences at the bottom of the list.
3.  Navigate to the Audio category in the left menu.
4.  Click Device to see your sound settings.
5.  Choose your Audio System. If you use a professional interface, select ASIO. If you use the standard computer speakers, select DirectSound or WASAPI.
6.  Select your input and output devices from the dropdown menus.
7.  Click Apply and then OK to save your choices.

## 🎛 Understanding The Interface

The main workspace shows four primary areas that you will use to create music:

*   The Arrange View: This large space in the middle holds your tracks and audio clips. You drag and drop sounds here to build your song.
*   The Tracks: These are the lines running top to bottom. Each track holds individual instruments like vocals, drums, or guitars.
*   The Mixer: This panel at the bottom shows faders. Use these to control the volume, balance, and effects for each track.
*   The Transport Bar: This area at the bottom holds the play, stop, record, and loop buttons. You control the playback of your project here.

## ⏺ Recording Audio

To record a vocal or instrument:

1.  Create a new track by double-clicking the empty area on the left side of the screen.
2.  Click the small red button on the track to arm it for recording.
3.  Check the meter to ensure your sound shows up as green or yellow. If the meter shows red, lower your volume.
4.  Click the main Record button on the bottom Transport bar.
5.  Perform your part.
6.  Click Stop on the Transport bar when finished.

## 📁 Saving And Exporting

Protect your work by saving your projects often:

1.  Click File in the top menu and select Save Project.
2.  Give your project a name and choose a folder. Organize your projects in a dedicated music folder to keep them safe.
3.  When you finish your song, select File, then Render.
4.  Choose your file format, such as WAV or MP3.
5.  Pick a location for the final file.
6.  Click Render File to create your music file.

## 💡 Best Practices

*   Save your project after every important change.
*   Keep your audio drivers updated through your hardware manufacturer's website.
*   Use folders to group tracks, such as keeping all drum parts in one folder.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What if I hear crackling sounds?
This usually means your buffer size is too low. Go to Preferences, Audio, and Device. Increase the buffer size until the sound clears.

Where do the program files install?
The installer puts files in the Program Files folder by default. Do not move these folders after install as it may break the links.

Can I move my project to another computer?
Yes. Copy the entire folder containing your project file and your audio recordings to a portable drive. Open the project file on the new machine from that drive.

How do I add effects?
Click the FX button on any track. Choose an effect from the list to add it to your audio. Use the sliders to change how the effect sounds.
